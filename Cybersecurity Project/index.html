Go The Distance: Fight for Data Privacy

In today's society, internet safety and data privacy have taken a backseat in people's minds as social media has made sharing online popular, and the internet's free services have lured people to sharing their data freely. This unchecked relationship with online media threatens to produce a generation and culture that prioritizes momentary gratification over a measured and safe approach to media.

The Problem

In cyberspace, online safety translates to data privacy since buying and selling data to companies holds tremendous value in today's economy. Today, the data collection industry has multipled and become bigger, and so has the hacking industry. In 2023, the Office for Civil Rights (OCR) reported a 239% escalation in hacking-related data breaches. Likewise, the National Public Data Breach that occurred in April 2024 impacted an astonishing 2.9 billion records exposing full names, social security numbers, mailing addresses, email addresses, and phone numbers. The Bureau of Justice Statistics notes “While about 6 in 10 (59%) victims of identity-theft incidents in 2021 had financial losses of $1 or more, those losses totaled $16.4 billion” which indicates the severity and unpredictability of cyberattacks. In short, data theft could cost people their finances, reputation, and security. Therefore, protecting our data should be a paramount concern that propels action.



Why don't people care about “online safety” anymore?

Human Desire for Connection

Indifference to personal data privacy primarily stems from the human desire for the connections we form through online platforms like social media. Neil Sahota explores how humanity’s innate drive for social interactions causes us to sacrifice our digital privacy. He points out that “we all know (at least subconsciously) that our data is being taken, mined, and sold…[However, we’re] often copacetic with that because we get enough value [(social media connections)] out of the systems that we don’t mind this cost”.

Feelings of Powerlessness

The feeling of powerlessness over personal data has also hindered people’s motivation to protect themselves online. As technology integrates more into our daily routines, it feels increasingly difficult to keep our information and data safe. With platforms like Google, YouTube, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, etc, becoming staples in modern-day households, data privacy loss seems inevitable since it’s not possible to live life without these services. 81% of American adults report experiencing a lack of control over the data companies collect.

How do we fight back? (A.K.A Call To Action)

1.

Be Informed

Understanding digital privacy laws and your rights on different online platforms is an important step in protecting your privacy. In places where the law can't protect you, you can take steps to protect yourself by limiting your usage of certain platforms, etc.

2.

Walk Two Miles

Take steps to limit unnecessary data sharing and strengthen data protection . For example, not downloading from random websites, creating strong and new passwords for your accounts, and using two-factor authentication are great places to start. Although these steps can add some inconvenience in our life, this extra mile will help protect you from future disasters.

3.

Join Organizations

There are many organizations like "Go The Distance" throughout the U.S. who champion for more and new Digital Privacy Rights. Joining them will force authorities and companies to respect the wishes of the public and help meaningful laws and regulations (like the the Privacy Rights Act) to be passed in what can be collected by online platform companies.

4.

Teach Others

Do your part to inform others about the dangers that lurk in neglecting their data privacy. Data citizenship enables everyone to enjoy the internet in healthy and responsible ways. Therefore, the population must be reminded and encouraged to take control of their digital safety and footprint. By reaching out to family, friends, and community members, you can make a difference in keeping people safe!
